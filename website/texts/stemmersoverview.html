We present stemming algorithms, and Snowball stemmers, for Russian, for the Romance languages French, Spanish, Portuguese and Italian, and for German and Dutch.

Note that by i-suffix we mean inflexional suffix, and by d-suffix, derivational suffix.

There are also Snowball stemmers for English (believed to be an improvement on the 1980 ‘porter’ algorithm), for the Scandinavian languages Swedish, Norwegian (bokmål dialect) and Danish, and for the Schinke/Willett Latin algorithm, and the Kraaij/Pohlmann Dutch algorithm. Some of these will be released in due course.

Each formal algorithm should be compared with the corresponding Snowball program.

Surprisingly, the French stemmer turns out to be the most complicated, whereas the Russian stemmer, despite its large number of suffixes, is very simple. In fact it is interesting that English, with its minimal use of i-suffixes, has such a complex stemmer. This is partly due to the delicate nature of i-suffix removal (undoubling the p after removing ing from hopping etc), and partly to the wealth of forms of d-suffixes, deriving as they do from the mixed Romance and Germanic ancestry of the language.
